The Last Lamplighter

In a city that had forgotten the stars, an old lamplighter named Oren still climbed the iron stairs each dusk to light the final gas lamp on Hollow Hill. The factories below had long since traded flame for cold electric glare, and the people no longer looked up. But Oren remembered a promise made to his father: as long as one true light still burned, the city would not lose its way.

One grey evening, as snow settled on his shoulders, Oren found the old lamp dark and its glass cracked. A young girl waited beside it, her eyes bright with a question. "Why do you bother," she asked, "when no one watches anymore?" Oren only smiled, struck his match, and coaxed the flame back to life. In its warm glow, the girl saw — for the first time — her own shadow stretch long and brave across the snow.

That night the lamp burned steadier than it had in years. And when Oren finally descended the hill, he left the matches in the girl's small hands. Some lights, he knew, were never meant to be carried alone.
